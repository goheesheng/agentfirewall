Problem 02 / 10
02 · Problem

An agent's keypair signs
whatever it's asked to.

x402 turned every HTTP endpoint into a payable surface. Agents now sign stablecoin payments at the speed of API calls — with no spending limits and no way to verify the price the seller actually committed to. Two weeks ago, a prompt-injected agent transferred $200,000 from a wallet on Base.

"my x402 wallet just autopaid for a vpn subscription i forgot i even had. it didn't flinch."
— @ClankerOnChain · May 7, 2026
"PaymentRequirements.amount is set unilaterally by the seller. A malicious endpoint can publish $0.01 in the Bazaar and charge $1.00 in the live 402."
— x402 architectural critique, Layer 1 #5
The blast radius is growing. 35M tx on Solana, $600M annualized — and zero spending controls at the keypair layer.
Solution 03 / 10
03 · Solution

AgentFirewall.

An in-process proxy that holds your agent's signing key and runs two checks on every payment — spend policy and Ed25519 quote-binding. Both must pass, or nothing signs.

agent→ AgentFirewall→ budget?→ discovery?→ sig verify?→ price match?→ sign & pay·or· HTTP 451
LOCK 01 · SPEND POLICY

Per-call cap · daily budget · seller allow-list.

Stops Grok-style prompt-injection drains. Five named templates (paranoid → open). Daily budget rolls at UTC midnight.

LOCK 02 · QUOTE-BINDING

Ed25519 verify of seller-attested prices.

Fetches signed .well-known/x402.json, verifies signature, compares discovery price to live 402 price. Multi-facilitator.

Product · live demo 06 / 10
06 · Product

Five calls. Three blocks.
One QUOTE_LIE caught.

Demo agent fires 5 calls through the firewall. Two pass. Three are blocked at the keypair, before any signature is produced. The signed-quote check is the centerpiece.

✓ ALLOWdata-seller-a · $0.0050 · sig 32vcQJVH…A1nRKi verified
✓ ALLOWdata-seller-b · $0.0080 · sig 52dfaJ2z…MxdZ1t verified
✗ BLOCKBUDGET_EXCEEDED
Per-call cap is $0.0100 USDC, requested $0.0500.
✗ BLOCKUNKNOWN_SELLER
Host evil.example.com not in allowedSellerHosts.
✗ BLOCKQUOTE_LIE
Seller's signed discovery price $0.0100 ≠ live 402 price $1.0000. Signature verified, mismatch detected.
The QUOTE_LIE moment: the malicious seller signed $0.01 with a real Ed25519 key in their .well-known/x402.json, then quoted $1.00 live. The signature proves the lie. Without quote-binding, a single agent call burns 100× the expected amount.
