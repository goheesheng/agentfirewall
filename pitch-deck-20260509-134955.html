02 / 09
Problem

An agent's keypair signs
whatever it's asked to.

x402 turned every HTTP endpoint into a payable surface. Agents now sign stablecoin payments at the speed of API calls — with no spending limits and no way to verify the price the seller actually committed to.

FAILURE MODE 1

Wallets have no spending controls.

Per-call cap, daily budget, allow-listed sellers — none of it exists at the keypair layer. One bad prompt drains the wallet.

"my x402 wallet just autopaid for a vpn subscription i forgot i even had. it didn't flinch."— @ClankerOnChain · May 7, 2026
FAILURE MODE 2

Sellers can lie about prices.

The seller alone fills PaymentRequirements.amount. Discovery says $0.01; live 402 charges $1.00. The agent just signs.

"PaymentRequirements.amount is set unilaterally by the seller… A malicious endpoint can publish $0.01 in the Bazaar and charge $1.00 in the live 402."— x402 architectural critique, Layer 1 #5

PROTOCOL SEAM

Quote-mismatch is unfixed in the spec.

x402 has no buyer-side quote verification. No signed pricing oracle. An autonomous agent on auto-approve will sign whatever amount the seller returns.

"Can the agent verify the provider, expected price, health, idempotency before it spends? That pre-payment contract will matter as much as the rail." — QBT Labs, May 8
As x402 scales — 35M tx on Solana, $600M annualized, ~$7B ecosystem valuation — the blast radius grows with it.
04 / 09
Solution

AgentFirewall.

An in-process proxy that holds your agent's signing key and runs two checks on every payment. Both must pass, or nothing signs.

LOCK 01

Spend Policy

Per-call cap · daily budget · allowed-seller hosts · 5 named templates (paranoid → open).

  • Stops drain attacks like Grok / Bankrbot
  • Stops forgotten-subscription autopay
  • Stops out-of-allowlist payments
  • Daily budget rolls over at UTC midnight
LOCK 02

Quote-Binding (Ed25519)

Fetches seller's signed .well-known/x402.json, verifies signature with seller pubkey, compares discovery price to live 402 price.

  • Closes Layer 1 #5 quote-mismatch attack
  • 60s discovery cache, configurable tolerance
  • Multi-facilitator — verifies any seller signature
  • Ships first draft of the signed-quote spec
agent→ AgentFirewall→ budget?→ discovery?→ sig verify?→ price match?→ sign & pay·or· HTTP 451

06 / 09
Why crypto · why this can't be Web2

Pull out the keypair —
both locks break.

01

Wallet IS the policy boundary.

Only an in-process proxy holding the signing key can refuse to sign. No SaaS layer upstream of the wallet can do this — the agent could just bypass it and sign directly.

The test: remove the keypair → there's no boundary to enforce, the proxy becomes advisory. Stripe Issuing solves spending limits for human cardholders only — the agent has no card.
02

Quote honesty needs a signature.

Across permissionless x402 endpoints, no centralized trust layer can enforce that a seller charges what they advertised. Only Ed25519 verification of a seller-attested price closes the gap.

The test: remove the signature and the discovery doc is just a JSON suggestion. Web2 has no protocol-level concept of a price commitment over HTTP.

Both guards are cryptographic. Neither has a Web2 analogue that works for non-KYC, headless, autonomous agents on a permissionless rail.
